Title: Aspirin Side Effects and Contraindications

Aspirin is commonly used for pain relief and cardiovascular protection. However, it can cause several side effects including stomach irritation, gastric ulcers, and increased bleeding risk. Contraindications include active peptic ulcer disease, severe liver disease, and allergy to salicylates. Patients should be monitored for signs of gastrointestinal bleeding when on long-term aspirin therapy.
